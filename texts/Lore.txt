I all began in the Saurian Senate.

The Gekkos had accused the Dragons of seeking disproportionate representation in the Senate, to become a monopoly on power.

The Dragons were sooth and suave. Their honeyed words convinced the other Saurians - the Iguanas, Salamanders, the ever-fickle Chameleons - that if the Senate were to become majority Dragon, then it can only mean that Dragons are the best to lead, since the Senate only ever admits those most qualified.

The Gekkos were aghast at their Saurian brethren, and, as their number continued to fall under the sway of the power-hungry Dragons, the Gekkos formed a Resistance with the races the Dragons deemed inferior: the Frogs and Toads, the Serpents, and even the fearsome Spider Clans.

Until one day, in the Senate, the leader of the Dragons, Balthazar Draginius, gave a rousing speech, a rallying cry for the loyal Saurians, in which he denounced the Gekko Faction as mentally deficient, and that only those with true wisdom may lead the Saurian races. The speech ended with a single statement that was heard round the world of Saurinius, and would forever change the course of Saurian history. The insult that began the Saurian War:

The Gekkos are Unhinged.

At this, the Gekko delegation sprang forth in anger, accusing Balthazar of slander, of incitement, of dividing the Saurian races. Not all Gekkos were so civil, though. Fringe members of the Resistance were in attendance, and attacked Balthazar, who, incredibly, ended up losing his head in the ensuing fight.

In another astonishing turn of events, the Draconic Army arrived almost immediately at the scene. Some astute Gekkos theorised that the event itself was staged. Those Gekkos were ground under. Only time will tell.

One thing is certain. War is coming.
